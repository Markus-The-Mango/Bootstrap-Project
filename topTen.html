My Top Ten

#1 VelociCoaster

When in line for this ride I didn't think it could possibly impress me as much as it did. but the sharp turns, low to the ground elements, and amazing theming where enough to get me.

#2 magnum xl-200

Maybe its the short lines, amazing view, or the amazing elements like the triangle hills or pretzle. but this ride holds a special place in my heart.

#3 Raptor

I lvoe this classic B&M, the ride is smooth but still jolts you around as it traverses its complex layout, every time I ride I have an amazing experience.

#4 The Beast

Though it's rough, this ride is perfectly fun to ride and going through the natural landscape makes it such an enjoyable experience. In the end this ride really is a beast.

#5 Forest flyer

Theirs a lot to say for adventure and risk, which is what you get on mountain coasters such as forest flyer where you take the ride at your own speed with little interference.

#6 Backlot Stunt

This ride is very over looked by many but once on you experience an overall great ride with an insane vertical corkscrew and a great fire element that really makes you feel the heat.

#7 Hagrid's Magical Creatures Motorbike Adventure

I wouldn't consider myself a Harry potter fan but this was an amazing experience, the theming was on point and the motercycle cars were fun. plus the many tricks the layout plays are overall amazing.

#8 great pumpkin coaster

This is a very small ride that I only added becuase of its amazing team that put up with my friend and I riding it over and over again and fake screaming on the tiny hill.

#9 Steel Vengance

While this ride is often a little over rated in my opinion it is still a nice ride. The structure is glorious and makes you feel small as you walk past and the loud lift chain just furthers the clasic coaster experience.

#10 Sea Dragon

Though this ride is fairly simple and overs very little in theming or elements, the historic ride still deserves a mention since it is one of few tradional manual operated rides and really stood to the time.
